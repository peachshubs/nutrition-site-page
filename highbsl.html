Want a quick way to lower your glucose levels?


Here's some snack ideas for you to munch on!
(We mentioned the carbohydrates as well.)



Living with diabetes doesn't mean you have to miss out on
snacking. On the contrary, snacking can play a pivotal role
in managing your blood sugar levels and keeping your
energy steady throughout the day. In essence, diabetic
patients can enjoy an array of snacks that not only taste
good but also help maintain stable blood sugar levels.
Always remember, the key is to focus on options that are
high in fibre, protein, and healthy fats and to be mindful of
portion sizes and overall carbohydrate intake.



It’s important to consider quantity and quality when it comes
to carbs. Binging on carbohydrates or having too many
simple carbohydrates at once can cause spikes in blood
sugar, which in turn can increase the risk of diabetes
complications like diabetic neuropathy, diabetic retinopathy,
heart disease, and stroke.

• Walking
You don’t need a gym membership or expensive exercise equipment
to get moving. If you have a supportive pair of shoes and a safe
place to walk, you can start today.

• Cycling
Roughly half of people with type 2 diabetes have arthritis. The two
conditions have several risk factors in common, including obesity.
Diabetic neuropathy, a condition that causes nerve damage, can
also cause joint pain in people with type 2 diabetes. If you have joint
pain, consider choosing low impact activities.

• Pilates
Pilates is a popular fitness program to help improve core strength,
coordination, and balance. According to a 2020 study of older adult
women with type 2 diabetes, it may also help improve blood sugar
management.

Regular physical activity is important — not only for managing type 2
diabetes but also for promoting your overall health. If you have any other
health conditions in addition to type 2 diabetes, talk with your doctor
before starting a new fitness routine. They can help you learn how to
stay safe and minimize your risk of injury while meeting your fitness goals.



How can hyperglycemia be treated in everyday life?

• Take all your medications exactly as prescribed.

• Learn all you can about diabetes. Make healthy eating and physical activity
part of your daily routine. Maintain a healthy weight. Monitor your blood
sugar, and follow your health care provider's instructions for managing your
blood sugar level. Take your medications as directed by your health care
provider. Ask your diabetes treatment team for help when you need it.

• Always have emergency insulin on hand. Educate your loved ones on how
and when to use it.

• Like diabetes, high blood pressure can damage your blood vessels. High
cholesterol is a concern, too, since the resulting damage is often worse
and more rapid when you have diabetes. When these conditions team up,
they can lead to a heart attack, stroke or other life-threatening conditions.

• If you're stressed, it's easy to neglect your usual diabetes care routine.
To manage your stress, set limits. Prioritize your tasks. Learn relaxation
techniques. Get plenty of sleep. And above all, stay positive. Diabetes care
is within your control. If you're willing to do your part, diabetes won't stand
in the way of an active, healthy life.
